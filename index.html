Top 3 activities to do at Lethbridge

Hiking

Lethbridge offers a variety of scenic trails for hiking, showcasing its unique landscapes and natural beauty. Here are a few notable trails:

  • Helen Schuler Nature Centre Trails: Located along the Oldman River, these trails are perfect for nature enthusiasts, offering easy, family-friendly paths with great views of the river valley, wildlife, and educational signage about the local ecosystem.
  • Indian Battle Park Trails: Winding through the historic river valley, these trails combine gentle paths with more rugged sections, providing a mix of history and nature. Hikers can explore the site of the 1870s Battle of the Belly River while enjoying lush greenery.
  • Nicholas Sheran Park Loop: A peaceful urban park trail around a scenic lake, ideal for casual strolls. This short, flat loop offers views of waterfowl and opportunities for picnicking and relaxing.
Lethbridge's trails cater to both casual walkers and seasoned hikers, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Festivals

Lethbridge hosts a vibrant array of festivals throughout the year, celebrating culture, art, and community. Here are a few highlights:

  • Lethbridge Folk Festival: This annual event showcases local and international folk music artists, featuring multiple stages and a variety of genres. It's a family-friendly festival with workshops, food vendors, and a lively atmosphere.
  • Heritage Days: Celebrating the diverse cultures that make up Lethbridge, this festival offers food, music, and performances from various cultural groups. It’s a great opportunity to experience the city’s rich multicultural heritage.
  • Lethbridge International Film Festival: This festival highlights independent films from around the world, providing a platform for filmmakers and a chance for audiences to engage with diverse cinematic perspectives.
  • Lethbridge Jazz & Blues Festival: Featuring local and national artists, this festival presents a mix of jazz, blues, and related genres in various venues around the city, promoting live music and community engagement.
These festivals reflect Lethbridge's lively cultural scene and offer residents and visitors alike a chance to connect and celebrate.

Food Tours

Lethbridge offers a delightful culinary scene, with food tours that showcase the city's diverse flavors and local ingredients. Here are some popular food tour options:

  • Lethbridge Food Tours: This guided tour takes participants through the city’s best eateries, featuring tastings at local restaurants, cafes, and specialty shops. Guests can enjoy a mix of international cuisines and learn about the chefs and culinary traditions behind the dishes.
  • Farm-to-Table Tours: Highlighting local agriculture, these tours often include visits to nearby farms where participants can sample fresh produce and artisanal products. They focus on sustainable practices and the importance of local sourcing.
  • Craft Brewery Tours: Explore Lethbridge’s burgeoning craft beer scene with a tour of local breweries. Participants can enjoy tastings and learn about the brewing process while sampling a variety of unique beers.
  • Culinary Events and Festivals: Throughout the year, Lethbridge hosts food festivals that celebrate local cuisine, offering tastings, cooking demonstrations, and opportunities to meet local chefs.
These tours provide a tasty way to experience Lethbridge's vibrant food culture and connect with the community.
